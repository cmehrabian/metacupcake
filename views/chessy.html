Episode 1, Andrea Lopez (Feat. Cameron Mehrabian)

May 13, 2014 by Michael Betts, Jake Tully

Andrea Lopez, a freshman at the University of California, Santa Cruz, has been writing and singing her own songs since she was 13 years old. She mostly writes her songs in her dorm room, drawing influence from the beautiful views and fun interactions she has with her friends.

Lopez’ own style of sing-songwriting is very controlled - it’s a sort of performance that paints Lopez as wise beyond her own years. Her penchant for lyricism is vividly apparent from a first listen alone, as she takes her writing incredibly seriously.

“I’ve been writing journals since elementary school. What convinced me was Anne Frank. I’m on journal number nine now.”

The entirety of Lopez’ musical output is original work, with influences drawing from Death Cab for Cutie and Best Coast. One of Lopez’ favorite things to do is to listen to entire albums on long drives and fully digest the music.

“People think I sound like Regina Spektor,” said Lopez. “I’m very different lyrically.”


Lopez said she loves sharing music. She loves listening to math and experimental rock, despite both of those genres being wildly unlike her own music. When asked what three items Lopez would bring into a room if locked in it for 24 hours, Lopez replied, “A journal with a pen, a guitar and candy.” What truly is outstanding about Lopez’ character is her ability to impart a fun time and dismiss the the solemn nature that often goes hand in glove with many singer-songwriters.

Andrea is a QT! When asked about any pet peeves she had, Andrea replied:

“I have an opposite of a pet peeve, I love the sound of people eating pickles,” said Lopez. “I also love the sound of people eating apples.”

Be on the lookout for this rising treasure, as Andrea Lopez continues to write songs about her life experiences, eating candy the whole way.
